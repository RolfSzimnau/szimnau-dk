Twice a year I'd go into the app and adjust the outdoor light timer. Once in spring when 21:00 was already pushing dusk. Once in autumn when it was dark at 17:30 and the lights weren't coming on until an hour later. I never quite got it right — there's always a week or two where the schedule is visibly wrong, lights flicking on in broad daylight or leaving the garden dark for an hour after sunset.

The fix is obvious in retrospect: stop using clock times and use the sun instead.

## The setup

WiZ flood lights on the front of the house — two units. The **WiZ integration** is built into HA; it discovers bulbs automatically on the local network, no HACS needed.

## The two automations

The on-trigger uses HA's built-in sun platform with an offset:

```yaml
alias: Flood lights — Sunset on
trigger:
  - platform: sun
    event: sunset
    offset: "+00:15:00"
action:
  - service: light.turn_on
    target:
      entity_id: light.flood_lights
    data:
      brightness_pct: 100
```

The 15-minute offset means the lights come on after it's actually dark — not the moment the sun technically sets, when there's still plenty of ambient light.

The off-trigger is a fixed time. Midnight is the right call here: the lights serve a practical purpose at night, but there's no reason to run them until morning.

```yaml
alias: Flood lights — Midnight off
trigger:
  - platform: time
    at: "00:00:00"
action:
  - service: light.turn_off
    target:
      entity_id: light.flood_lights
```

## The dashboard

A pair of Mushroom template chips showing the next trigger time for each automation. The sunset time is calculated from the sun entity so it updates daily without any manual input:

```yaml
- type: custom:mushroom-chips-card
  chips:
    - type: template
      icon: mdi:weather-sunset
      content: >
        On: {{ (as_timestamp(state_attr('sun.sun','next_setting'))
             + 15 * 60) | timestamp_custom('%H:%M') }}
    - type: template
      icon: mdi:weather-night
      content: "Off: 00:00"
```

In Denmark the sunset shift between June and December is about six hours. The lights track that automatically — as late as around 22:15 in summer, as early as around 15:30 in mid-December. Neither requires touching the automation.

## What changed

The two transition weeks in March and October where the schedule was always wrong are gone. It's not a complicated automation — two triggers, two actions — but it's one of those things that makes the house feel like it actually knows what time of year it is.
