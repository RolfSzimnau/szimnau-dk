For about two weeks after installing the Sentinel camera, I had motion notifications turned on. Every notification said "Motion detected." Birds, shadows, a plastic bag blowing past the lens at 3am. I turned them all off and left the camera sitting there doing nothing useful for another month.

The fix was two things in the right order: LLM Vision for descriptions, and `armed_away` as the only condition that actually matters.

## What the integration gives you

After adding the camera, HA creates a set of binary sensors from the onboard detection:

```
binary_sensor.sentinel_motion
binary_sensor.sentinel_person
binary_sensor.sentinel_animal
binary_sensor.sentinel_vehicle
```

Person and vehicle come from the camera's own AI chip. They're reasonably accurate — better than pure motion, worse than perfect. The animal sensor has caught actual foxes and also, memorably, a large plastic bag moving in a way that apparently seemed alive.

## LLM Vision

The [LLM Vision](https://github.com/valentinfrlch/ha-llmvision) integration pulls a snapshot from the camera and sends it to a language model with a prompt. The response comes back as a string you can use in a notification.

```yaml
service: llmvision.image_analyzer
data:
  provider: openai
  model: gpt-4o-mini
  message: >
    Describe what you see in one sentence, under 20 words.
    Focus on who or what is present and what they're doing.
  image_entities:
    - camera.sentinel
  max_tokens: 60
response_variable: ai_result
```

The quality varies. "Person walking toward door carrying package" is great. "A figure is visible in the image near a structure" is less great. On average it's good enough to tell at a glance whether a notification is worth opening.

I toggle it with `input_boolean.sentinel_ai_analysis`. When it's off, notifications still go out — just with a generic message and the snapshot. The toggle exists because I don't need AI descriptions at 2am when the notification is already waking me up; I need them during the day when I'm glancing at my phone in a meeting.

## The only condition that matters

Everything goes through one check: `alarm_control_panel.home_alarm` state `armed_away`. If I'm home, no notifications. Full stop.

```yaml
alias: Sentinel — Person alert
trigger:
  - platform: state
    entity_id: binary_sensor.sentinel_person
    to: "on"
condition:
  - condition: state
    entity_id: alarm_control_panel.home_alarm
    state: armed_away
action:
  - choose:
      - conditions:
          - condition: state
            entity_id: input_boolean.sentinel_ai_analysis
            state: "on"
        sequence:
          - service: llmvision.image_analyzer
            data:
              provider: openai
              model: gpt-4o-mini
              message: Describe what you see in one sentence, under 20 words.
              image_entities: [camera.sentinel]
              max_tokens: 60
            response_variable: ai_result
          - service: notify.all_devices
            data:
              title: "Person at camera"
              message: "{{ ai_result.response_text }}"
              data:
                image: /api/camera_proxy/camera.sentinel
    default:
      - service: notify.all_devices
        data:
          title: "Person at camera"
          message: "Check the snapshot."
          data:
            image: /api/camera_proxy/camera.sentinel
```

## From 5 automations to 3

I had accumulated five automations around this camera:
- Motion alert (any motion, always firing)
- Person alert
- A separate automation that called LLM Vision and stored the result
- A night suppression automation that turned off alerts between midnight and 7am
- A rate-limiter to avoid burning through API credits

The problem wasn't any one automation — it was that they were all partially in charge of the same decision. Whether to notify. The night suppression sometimes conflicted with the armed_away logic. The rate-limiter was a blunt instrument that would occasionally silence a legitimate alert.

I deleted all five and started from scratch with three:

1. **Person alert** (the one above) — handles the AI choice internally with `choose`
2. **Vehicle alert** — simpler, no AI, just snapshot + notification
3. **AI toggle reset** — runs at midnight, turns `sentinel_ai_analysis` back on if it was manually switched off during the day

The night suppression is gone because `armed_away` already handles it — if I'm asleep at home, I'm not armed away. The rate limiter is gone because the AI toggle does that job more deliberately.

## The dashboard card

```yaml
type: vertical-stack
cards:
  - type: custom:advanced-camera-card
    cameras:
      - camera_entity: camera.sentinel
    live:
      show_image_during_load: true
    view:
      default: live
  - type: custom:mushroom-chips-card
    chips:
      - type: entity
        entity: binary_sensor.sentinel_person
        icon: mdi:account
        icon_color: red
      - type: entity
        entity: binary_sensor.sentinel_vehicle
        icon: mdi:car
      - type: entity
        entity: input_boolean.sentinel_ai_analysis
        icon: mdi:brain
        tap_action:
          action: toggle
  - type: logbook
    entities:
      - binary_sensor.sentinel_person
      - binary_sensor.sentinel_vehicle
    hours_to_show: 24
```

The logbook at the bottom is the part I actually use most. After a trip away I scroll through the 24-hour log to see what triggered while I was out. It's less anxiety-inducing than replaying push notifications one by one.
